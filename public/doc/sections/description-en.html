ERA vocabulary: Description back to ToC

Figure 1 shows the main classes and properties of this vocabulary, which represent concepts related to the railway infrastructure and to the vehicles authorized to operate over it.


This vocabulary is divided into two main portions: (1) Railway Infrastructure (presented at the top of the figure), and (2) Railway Vehicles (presented at the bottom of the figure).

  • The Railway Infrastructure portion contains two layers: the Topological layer where the network topological concepts are defined, and the Implementation layer which represents sections of line, operational points, tracks with all of their related concepts, as well other physical infrastructure elements. Additionally the vocabulary reuses the geosparql pattern for the representation of all infrastructure and topological objects as geographical features that have a certain geometry, e.g. coordinates.

  • The Railway Vehicles portion contains information on the vehicles and their keepers, and of the vehicle types, their manufacturers, and their safety certificates.

The vocabulary is also aligned with routebook concepts from Appendix D2 in Comission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2019/773 of 16 May 2019 on the technical specification for interoperability relating to the operation and traffic management subsystem of the rail system within the European Union and repealing Decision 2012/757/EU. Routebook classes include signals (era:Signal), and switches (era:Switch). Most routebook concepts are represented as properties. Examples are era:operatingLanguage that is a list of (SKOS) predefined values and corresponds to routebook concept "3.5.1 Operating language". Another property is era:hasWalkway that indicates whether a tunnel has a walkway and corresponds to routebook concept "3.2.3 Tunnels: location, name, length, specific information such as the existence of walkways and points of safe egress ...". Some of the concepts in the vocabulary are also RINF such as era:SectionOfLine which corresponds to routebook concept "2.1.1 Line sections". Another example of a RINF parameter that is also a routebook concept is era:etcsDegradedSituation that is RINF 1.1.1.3.10.1 and routebook concept "3.1.7 Means of Communication with the traffic management/control centre in normal, degraded and emergency situation". All concepts that have been implemented refer to appendix D2 with the annotation property era:routebookIndex.

The vocabulary also includes all those national railway (era:NationalRailwayProfile) parameters described in appendix D3 "ERTMS trackside engineering information relevant to operation that the infrastructure manager shall provide to the railway undertaking". An example of a trackside engineering property is era:dNvovtrp that represents the maximum distance for overriding the train trip and corresponds to appendix D3 concept 1.5.5. All concepts that have been implemented refer to appendix D3 with the annotation property era:ertmsTracksideIndex. Some of these parameters are detailed in Subset 26, chapter 7 .

Railway Infrastructure Implementation Layer

An era:OperationalPoint is any location that is relevant for train service operations, such as those where train services may begin and end or change route, and where passenger or freight services may be provided. The era:SectionOfLine is the part of the line between adjacent operational points. These adjacent operational points are defined by the two properties era:opStart and era:opEnd, which indicate the beginning and the end of a section of line. A section of line may consist of one or several tracks; and also the operational point is related to one or more tracks. Both relationships are represented by the property era:track. A era:Track is any running track used for train service movements, and the era:Siding is any track within an operational point, which is not used for the operational routing of a train. An operational point is related to a siding through the property era:siding.

Railway Infrastructure Topological Layer

Operational points, sections of lines, and tracks have an abstraction (era:hasAbstraction) in the Topological layer. The abstraction allows a graph-based representation of these elements as nodes in the topological network (era:netElement). A net element may have finer grained net elements (era:elementPart). Net relations (era:netRelation) represent the edges in the topological network and point to net element A (era:elementA) and net element B (era:elementB) which are the two edge nodes. For each net relation, the navigability (era:navigability) and the position of nodes A and B (era:positionOnA) and (era:positionOnB) are represented. Railway Vehicles


An era:Vehicle belongs to an era:VehicleType that has been authorized to operate on the European railway infrastructure. The vehicle is owned (era:vehicleKeeper) by a era:VehicleKeeper. A vehicle type is manufactured (era:manufacturer) by an era:Manufacturer. Both manufacturers and vehicle keepers are subclasses of org:Organization.
